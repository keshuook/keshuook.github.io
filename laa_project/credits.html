The idea of a non-human culprit was inspired by “The Murders in the Rue Morgue”, a short story written by Edgar Allen Poe.

More Info

The idea was to guide the user through a bunch of clues:

  1. The first person may seem like a suspect since they have a seemingly aggressive dog and a expensive items, possibly stolen from the dead person, who must be wealthy since they have a diamond ring. This person then states they were sleeping and woke up due to hearing glass shatter.
  2. The second person seems to be a baker from the donut shop behind who says the dead person loves donuts.
  3. The third person is a fisherman who says they sold two fish to the dead person.
  4. The fourth person has a gun so could be a suspect but the first person heard glass shatter and not a gun firing so if we trust the first person, it was not a gun. This person states the dead person hunted a deer.

Within the scene itself:

  1. The broken aquarium must have been the glass shattering so the first person must be telling the truth. The aggressive looking dog and expensive items must not be related to the death.
  2. The gun must not have been an instrument for murder as it seems the dead person uses it for hunting, even the deer's head is hung above that the fourth person said they hunted. Fourth person can be trusted since first person could be trusted and they must know about the hunting hobby of the dead person since they seem like a hunter themselves.
  3. There is a donut but it is seemingly irrelevant.
  4. The food bowl, yarn and cat tree indicate that the dead person owned a cat, and we saw a cat outside.
  5. The food bowl is empty so the cat must have been hungry, it could have climbed the cat tree to reach the shelf upon which the aquarium was on to eat the fish and must have knocked it onto the owner by accident, taking the second fish to eat and running out.
